Social Control Theory - What is it?

Social control theory is a theory on why people don't commit crimes.
  Instead of answering the question "Why?" It aims to answer the question "Why not?"





What it means

Social control theory works when individuals have strong bonds with society.
If someone creates a connection within society, they are generally less likely to commit a crime. Where as if someone is disconnected or secluded from society they are more likely to commit a crime.
The lack of social bonds does not cause crime, but instead, creates and environment where crime is more likely to occur.
